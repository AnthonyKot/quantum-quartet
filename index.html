The Quantum Quartet

Four authors, four registers, the same physics — read side by side.

Between 1926 and 1935, four books appeared that taught the world how to think about quantum mechanics. Max Born brought the German tradition of precision and calculation. Paul Dirac distilled the subject to its logical bones. Richard Feynman rebuilt it from scratch, starting with the double-slit experiment. Lev Landau and Evgeny Lifshitz forged a single coherent mathematical edifice.

Each author enters the material from a different angle — experiment, axiom, calculation, thought experiment — and each leaves something out. This site reads them together, chapter by chapter, without ranking them against one another.
